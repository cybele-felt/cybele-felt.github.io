
# General Variables in Wet Felting  

Wet felting is a complex process, allowing fibers to bind to each
other through a physical process of felting which agitates and moves
the fibers and allows the scales (barbs) in the wool fibers to mesh
with each other. 

The felting process has many variables that have
been utilized by crafts people and artists over the years to produce
different results. We can consider these variables in four categories,
based on the:

1. process used in production of fiber
2. variability within the specific breed of sheep, goat or camelids 
3. variability between breeds
4. process used in wet-felting


We will examine each group in detail later, but this document provides
a general overview.

## Process used in production of fiber

- Cleaning process: temperature of water, chemicals, fiber
  manipulation, amount of lanolin left

- The carding process to produce roving or batting, breaking of fibers
  to produce more fiber ends, the orientation of wool (single
  direction in roving vs. random in batting). 

- Chemicals used in post process to coat the fibers and dye them


## Variability within the specific breed of sheep, goat or camelids 

- Domestication process: selection for wool quality, genetic diversity
  in the farm

- Labelling conventions for sheep/wool, availability of registry for
  the breeds

- Variability of wool quality within the animal, some animals have
  naturally many different quality of wool (eg. llama)

- Age, well-being, nutririon of the animal, winter vs. summer coats

## Variability between breeds


Variability between breeds is often attributed to many of the other
factors described above. It is important to distinguish between the
breed based criteria by using many different samples of the same breed
from different farms and sources. Beware of the differences of the
same breed in different countries. For example, Romney sheep in US is
very different than a Romney in UK. Also consider whether the sheep
breed is new to US or has been domesticated for a long time. Sheep
tend to adapt to the conditions of where they live and start to differ
as a result. Since 2020's, US has allowed the import of sperm and more
recently embryo from abroad. This means that some foreign breeds are
being introduced to US but their wool quality may change over time.

The main variability between breeds is understood along a number of
dimensions:

- Fiber thickness (fine, medium, coarse)
- Fiber length (short vs. long)
- Fiber composition (dual coated, hair and kemp mixed, crimp or not crimp)

In addition, there are a number of less documented properties such as
the structure of the scales which likely play a role as well.

## Process used in wet-felting

The process of wet-felting goes back to ancient times and is
surprisingly unchanged since then. Through experimentation, some
ancient and some recent, many variables have been discovered. We
outline a few here. Each will constitute their own chapter later.

- Layout: thickness of layout, directionality of the layout

- Process: soap used, temperature, agitation process, the amount and
  direction of manipulation

- Mixing of felting fibers: carding fibers together, layering
  different fibers horizontally, using different fibers in different
  parts of the layout

- Mixing with non-felting (or hard-felting) fibers, including
cellulose fibers (rayon, bamboo, tencel), protein based fibers (silk),
metal fibers. Additionally, using fabric and paper of many types in
felting.
